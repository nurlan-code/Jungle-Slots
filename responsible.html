Warning Signs to Watch For

Even though our games don't involve real money, it's important to recognize signs that gaming might be becoming problematic:

Time Management

Spending excessive time gaming, losing track of time, or neglecting responsibilities and relationships.

Preoccupation

Constantly thinking about gaming, planning next gaming sessions, or feeling restless when not playing.

Emotional Changes

Mood swings, irritability when unable to play, or using gaming to escape from problems or negative feelings.

Social Impact

Isolating from friends and family, lying about gaming habits, or prioritizing gaming over social activities.

Performance Issues

Declining performance at work or school, missing deadlines, or avoiding important tasks to game.

Loss of Interest

Losing interest in hobbies, activities, or things that were previously enjoyable.

Self-Assessment Quiz

Answer honestly to evaluate your gaming habits:

1. How often do you think about gaming when not playing?

2. Do you lose track of time while gaming?

3. Has gaming affected your relationships or responsibilities?

4. Do you feel restless or irritable when unable to play?

5. Do you use gaming to escape from problems or negative emotions?

Tools for Healthy Gaming

Use these strategies to maintain a healthy relationship with gaming:

Set Time Limits

Decide how much time you'll spend gaming before you start and stick to it.

Schedule Gaming

Plan gaming sessions like any other activity, ensuring balance with other responsibilities.

Use Reminders

Set alarms or notifications to remind yourself to take breaks and stop playing.

Stay Active

Maintain physical activities and hobbies outside of gaming to keep a balanced lifestyle.

Social Balance

Ensure gaming doesn't replace face-to-face social interactions with friends and family.

Protect Sleep

Avoid gaming close to bedtime to ensure quality sleep and rest.
